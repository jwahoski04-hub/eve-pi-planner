⬡ How to use the PI Planner
Plan EVE Online Planetary Interaction chains, track extraction timers, and optimize colony output across your characters.

What this tool does

The PI Planner helps you design and manage Planetary Interaction across EVE Online's four product tiers: P1 basic materials, P2 refined commodities, P3 specialized commodities, and P4 advanced commodities. It pulls live Jita pricing from Janice and your live colony data from the EVE ESI API.

The five tabs

  • Chain Calculator — pick one product, see its full schematic tree, inputs needed, and which tier is most profitable to sell at.
  • Multi-Product — select several products at once to see combined material requirements and a gap analysis against what your characters are already producing.
  • Planet Extraction Timers — countdown cards for every extractor across every character. Color-coded by urgency.
  • Characters — connect your EVE characters via ESI to auto-pull live colony data (extractors, factories, storage contents).
  • Fuel Block Production — calculate isotope-blend fuel block runs with PI + ice + market material cost breakdown.

Fastest way to get started

  • Go to Characters and click + Add Character. You'll be redirected to EVE SSO to authorize the read-only PI scope.
  • Once connected, your colonies auto-load. Head back to Chain Calculator.
  • Click Fetch Prices (Janice) to pull current Jita prices.
  • Click any product card to see its production chain and sell-point analysis.
  • Check the Planet Extraction Timers tab to see which extractors are about to expire.

No ESI? No problem

Every tab except Characters and Timers works fine without connecting an EVE account. You can plan chains, compare tiers, and calculate fuel runs purely from the schematic data. ESI adds the "what am I currently producing" layer.

What Chain Calculator does

Pick a single end product. The tool walks its full schematic tree backwards — every P3 that feeds it, every P2 that feeds those, every P1 that feeds those, down to P0 raw resources. Then it prices everything at Jita and tells you where the money is.

Step by step

  • Click Fetch Prices (Janice) once per session to load live Jita prices.
  • Adjust POCO Tax % if your export taxes aren't the 1% default.
  • Click any product card in the tier grid. P0 resources are hidden by default — filter by name in the search bar.
  • Use Output Qty to scale the whole chain. Inputs scale proportionally.

Sell Point Analysis

This is the key insight: for any chain, you have multiple places you could stop and sell. A P4 chain could be sold as P1, P2, P3, or finished P4. Each tier has different export tax (higher tier = more tax) and different market demand.

The analysis shows net revenue (after export tax) for each sell point. The BEST tag marks the most profitable option. Sometimes selling P2 intermediates is more profitable than finishing the P4 — the tool makes that math explicit.

Production Flow visualization

The horizontal flow diagram shows exactly how raw materials combine through each tier. Hover each node for quantities. Characters already producing an item show their portrait as a pip on that card.

Intermediate Schematics table

Lists every schematic run required, grouped by tier. Use this to plan how many factory planets you need and which schematics each should run.

What Multi-Product does

Chain Calculator is single-product. Multi-Product is for when you're trying to balance a portfolio — "if I'm producing Robotics AND Consumer Electronics AND Mechanical Parts, how do my colonies stack up?"

Selecting products

Click any cards to select them. The grid highlights your picks. The tables below update live as you add/remove products.

Gap Analysis panel

Shows per-item supply vs. demand. Green = you're over-producing this material. Red = you need more. This is computed from your actual ESI data (what your characters are currently producing) vs. what your selected end products require.

  • Toggle Hide sufficient to focus only on bottleneck items.
  • Click the ? next to the panel heading for the assumption details (factory counts, schematic cycles, etc.).

Max Sustainable Output panel

Answers the question: "what's the biggest X/day I can sustain of every selected product simultaneously, given my current raw material output?" Binary-searches for the ceiling and tells you which item is the binding constraint.

Combined Intermediates table

Merges all intermediate-tier requirements across your selected products. If two products both need Mechanical Parts, you see the total — useful when splitting production across alt characters.

What Extraction Timers shows

Every active extractor across every connected character, color-coded by urgency:

  • Green — plenty of time remaining.
  • Amber — extraction expiring within the amber threshold (default 72 hours).
  • Red — expiring within the red threshold (default 24 hours).
  • Expired — already ran out; the planet is sitting idle, losing income.

Tune the thresholds

The Amber under N hrs and Red under N hrs inputs at the top let you match the alerts to your playstyle. If you log in daily, set amber to 24. If you only check colonies weekly, set amber to 168.

Per-character grouping

Each character has its own collapsible section. Click the character name to collapse/expand. Cards show planet type, product being extracted, cycle length, and current countdown.

Live countdowns

Counters tick every second. If you leave this tab open in a browser tab, it's basically a live dashboard — no need to refresh.

Refresh button

Timer data comes from the cached ESI pull. Click ↻ Refresh to re-fetch PI data from EVE's servers — use this after you've extended extractors in-game.

Connecting a character

Click + Add Character. You'll be redirected to EVE Online's official SSO login page. Sign in with the character whose PI you want to track, then approve the esi-planets.manage_planets.v1 scope. You'll be redirected back and the character appears in the list.

What gets pulled

  • Every colony the character owns (planet type, ID, upgrade level).
  • Every pin on each planet (extractors, factories, launchpads, storage).
  • Schematic being run in each factory + extraction heads + expiry times.
  • Current storage contents (with volumes, for fill-percent calculations).

The scope is read-only. The tool cannot modify your PI, trade, or see anything else about your account. Tokens are stored in your browser's localStorage and never sent to any server except EVE's own SSO.

Managing characters

  • Click any character card to expand it and view their colonies.
  • Factory, extractor, launchpad, and storage counts are shown per planet, with storage fill-% bars.
  • Click the ✕ button on a character card to remove them (revokes local token storage — you'll need to re-authorize if you add them back).

Custom ESI app (optional)

The tool ships with a default client ID that works for everyone. If you'd rather use your own EVE Developer application, click ESI Setup and paste in your Client ID. The setup panel walks through creating an app at developers.eveonline.com with the correct callback URI.

Token refresh

Access tokens last 20 minutes; the tool silently refreshes using the stored refresh token. If refresh fails (revoked, expired), you'll need to re-add the character.

What Fuel Block Production does

Fuel blocks combine PI outputs (Coolant, Enriched Uranium, Mechanical Parts, Oxygen, Robotics) with ice products (isotopes, heavy water, liquid ozone) and strontium to produce POS/structure fuel. This tab calculates full cost vs. revenue for any batch size.

Step by step

  • Pick an isotope variant (Hydrogen, Helium, Nitrogen, Oxygen) — each corresponds to a different racial fuel block.
  • Set Runs — each run produces 40 blocks.
  • Click Fetch Prices to pull Jita prices for all materials and the output fuel block.

Reading the metrics

  • Blocks Produced — total output units (runs × 40).
  • Output Value — revenue if you sell the fuel blocks at Jita sell.
  • Total Materials — all PI + ice + market material cost combined.
  • Profit Margin — output minus materials. Green = profitable, red = loss.

PI vs. Ice/Market cost split

The summary breaks materials into two categories: PI (what you'd produce yourself) and Ice/Market (what you'd buy). If you run your own PI, the "PI Materials Cost" is an opportunity cost — you could sell that PI raw instead. The margin calculation assumes you buy everything at Jita for an apples-to-apples comparison.

General tips across the suite

  • Refresh prices often. Janice prices are cached by session — stale prices lead to bad decisions on thin-margin trades.
  • Check your POCO tax. 1% is for high-sec NPC POCOs with Customs Code Expertise V. Player POCOs can be 4-10%.
  • Extraction timers are your enemy. An expired extractor = zero income. Check the Timers tab before logging off.
  • Multi-Product + Gap Analysis is the most underused feature. If you're running PI on multiple alts, use it to find which raw materials are choking your throughput.
